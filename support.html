Support

Caltrak — food & nutrition tracking  ·  Privacy Policy

Caltrak is a free, on-device food and nutrition diary. There is no account, no signup, and no server-side backend — so support is direct from us.

  • Apple Health or Google Health Connect sync issues.
  • Questions about the AI features (bring-your-own-key for OpenAI, Anthropic, or Google AI).
  • Anything else.

We aim to reply within a few business days.

Common questions

Do I need an account?

No. Caltrak does not require an account, signup, or login. Every log entry stays on your device.

How do I delete my data?

All data is local. Uninstalling the app removes everything. Within the app you can also delete individual entries (swipe a row), and you can disconnect from Apple Health or Google Health Connect at any time from Settings → Integrations.

Do the AI features cost extra?

Caltrak does not charge for AI features and does not operate any servers. You provide your own API key from OpenAI, Anthropic, or Google AI Studio under Settings → AI Assistant, and your usage is billed by that provider directly.

I'm seeing the wrong serving size for a food.

Caltrak prefers a household serving (e.g. "1 medium" or "1 cup") when the food source provides one. For barcoded packaged products that don't carry a household label, the app falls back to the gram count from the label. Tap the "Servings / Grams" chip in the "Add to log" sheet to switch between views.

How do I sync with Apple Health?

Open Settings → Integrations and tap "Connect Apple Health". Caltrak writes each food entry, water log, and body-weight entry to HealthKit. Reads are filtered to Caltrak's own entries so other dietary-tracking apps don't double-count your totals.
